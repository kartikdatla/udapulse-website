What is a UDA?

A Unit of Dental Activity (UDA) is the accounting unit the NHS in England uses to value dental treatments and pay providers under the General Dental Services contract. A practice has a contracted number of UDAs to deliver each financial year (1 April – 31 March) and must deliver between 96% and 102% of that target to avoid clawback or wasted activity.

For plain-English explainers see the LDC Confederation "What is a UDA?" article and the BDA UDA Value Checker. For the official Band 2 sub-band guidance see NHSBSA.

How to prevent UDA clawback

Clawback occurs when an NHS dental contract delivers under 96% of its target UDAs and the shortfall is recovered from future NHS payments — typically between January and April. Five practical steps to prevent it:

  1. Track daily, not monthly. Monthly Compass reports are too slow to act on. Use a daily delivery view.
  2. Forecast per performer. Aggregating to clinic total hides under-performing associates whose shortfall drives the practice over the clawback line.
  3. Watch the daily-UDAs-required number. If pace dips and that figure climbs above your sustainable session rate, raise it with the associate or commissioner now — not in February.
  4. Use the 13-week early-warning window. Practices that flag risk by week 40 still have options; after week 48, choices narrow sharply.
  5. Contact the Local Area Team early. NHS England has discretion to allow carry-forward of a shortfall to the next year if the practice is confident it can make it up.

Glossary

UDA
Unit of Dental Activity. The accounting unit used by the NHS in England to value dental treatments and pay providers.
Course of Treatment (CoT)
All treatment a patient needs in one continuous episode of care. A CoT is what is banded and counts for UDAs, not each individual procedure.
Band 1 / 2a / 2b / 2c / 3
NHS England dental treatment bands. Band 1 = 1 UDA; Band 2a = 3; Band 2b = 5; Band 2c = 7; Band 3 = 12.
Clawback
NHS recovery of payment when a practice delivers under 96% of its contracted UDAs. Deducted from future payments — typically January to April.
Compass
NHSBSA Compass — the official web system for NHS dental contract management, payments and UDA delivery reporting.
FP17
The form NHS dentists submit per CoT to claim UDAs. Has a 62-day submission window from treatment completion.
NHSBSA
NHS Business Services Authority — the body that operates Compass and processes NHS dental payments.
GDS
General Dental Services contract — the standard NHS England dental contract type that uses UDAs.
PDS
Personal Dental Services contract — an alternative NHS England contract type, often with bespoke terms.
DCS127 / DCS129 / SD86c
NHSBSA Compass report types — schedule of payments, contract performance, and UDA delivery breakdowns.
NASDAL
National Association of Specialist Dental Accountants & Lawyers — the source of the dental industry benchmarks shown in UDA Pulse.
